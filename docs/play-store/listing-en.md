## Full description

**Double Bass Coach turns intonation practice into a game.**

Playing in tune is the hardest part of learning double bass — and practicing it is
usually the most boring. Double Bass Coach listens to your bass through your phone's
microphone and turns that practice into short games with scores, stars, streaks and
achievements, plus a coach that tells you one useful thing after every round.

**The games**
- **Find the Note** — land the note you see, right the first time. No needle to chase:
  the app scores the note you actually landed.
- **Long Notes** — hold one note steady and in tune, like the classic long-tone exercise.
- **Shifts** — move between positions on cue and land in tune, on one string or across
  strings.
- **Chords** — play a chord one note at a time, bottom to top, and hear whether your
  third and fifth sit in tune.

**A coach, not a grade**
After each round you get one observation in plain language — "most notes leaned sharp,
aim a touch lower" — the kind of thing a good teacher says. The Progress page shows how
in tune you're playing in words (Locked in / Solid / Developing), how this week compares
to last week, and which position deserves attention. Prefer raw numbers? Turn on
"Show technical details".

**Made for real practice**
- Prompts and results are readable from playing distance, bass in hand.
- Fixed-do (Do Ré Mi) or letter (C D E) note names — whatever your teacher uses.
- Arco and pizzicato, positions you choose (Simandl), pace settings from calm to swift.
- A built-in tuner for your open strings and a drone to practice against by ear.
- A two-minute setup teaches the app how *your* bass sounds on *your* phone.

**Private by design**
No account, no ads, no cloud, no telemetry. Your practice history stays on your phone
(with backup/restore to a file you control). Free software under the GPL — the source
code is linked in the app.

**Beta note:** the app has been tested on a limited set of phones and basses. If a note
isn't picked up right on your setup, you can record a practice report in the app and
email it to the developer — that's exactly how the app gets better.
